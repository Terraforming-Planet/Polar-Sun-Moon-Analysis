# Science and limitations

## Polar astronomy

Horizons provides calculated ephemerides based on maintained measurements and dynamical models.
They are not photographs. Equinox timestamps in this project are apparent geocentric Sun
declination zero crossings. Polar elevation is topocentric and airless. A 30-minute series brackets
the crossing and linear interpolation provides a sub-step timestamp; this interpolation uncertainty
must be considered when using the timestamp beyond this application's seasonal comparison purpose.

## Fire

FIRMS hotspots are thermal-anomaly observations. Fire radiative power, confidence and repeated
detections can support analysis, but a hotspot alone is not a fire-spread forecast. Spread modelling
also needs wind, fuel, terrain and validated incident data.

## Surface water

Optical water indices are affected by cloud, shadow, sediment, vegetation and ice. SAR can operate
through cloud but classifies radar backscatter rather than directly measuring depth. SWOT adds water
surface elevation, extent and slope. Volume needs bathymetry or a validated hypsometric relationship.

## Mountain water

Snow products, SMAP, GRACE-FO and InSAR constrain different components and scales. None provides a
direct high-resolution map of water in rock fractures. Electrical resistivity tomography,
magnetotellurics, seismics, boreholes and piezometers are suitable ground-validation families.

## Seafloor

Satellite altimetry and gravity infer broad bathymetric structure because seafloor mass perturbs the
ocean surface. Satellites do not directly image deep seafloor through seawater. Detailed bathymetry
requires acoustic surveys such as multibeam sonar; sub-bottom and electromagnetic methods answer
different geological questions and require in-water platforms.
